Privacy Notice

The OBS Assessment Tool runs entirely in your browser. Nothing you enter is sent to or stored on a server — only standard web-server access logs are kept.

In short: No backend, no accounts, no analytics, no runtime CDN. Your assessment is auto-saved only in your browser's localStorage, and you keep your own exported files. The server hosting this page retains only ordinary access logs (timestamps, IP, requested URL) — never the data you enter.

What data the tool handles

This tool is for consultants conducting information security assessments. The data you enter may include:

  • Your client's name, industry, and contact details
  • Your name, agency, and contact details
  • Answers to security-posture questions, which may reveal sensitive information about your client's controls, vulnerabilities, and risk profile
  • Free-text notes, narratives, evidence, and remediation plans

This is client-confidential information. Treat exported files accordingly.

Where data is stored

All data stays on your device. The vendored libraries (Chart.js, jsPDF) load from local disk, not a CDN. Data is held in two places:

LocationWhenFormat
Browser localStorageWhile a session is in progress (auto-save draft)Unencrypted JSON
Exported .json / .pdf / .csv fileWhen you use an Export buttonUnencrypted

What the server stores

The web server that delivers this page keeps only standard access logs — request timestamp, client IP address, and the URL requested — used for operational and security purposes. It does not receive or store any assessment data, answers, notes, or exported files, because the tool makes no data requests to the server.

Risks to be aware of

  • localStorage is unencrypted. Anyone with access to your browser profile — including other users of a shared workstation — can read the stored draft.
  • Exported files (JSON, PDF, CSV) are unencrypted. Restrict access, avoid unencrypted channels, and store them per your organisation's data-handling policy.

How to delete your data

What to deleteHow
Draft in the browserClear localStorage for this site in your browser's privacy/developer settings
Exported filesDelete the .json, .pdf, or .csv files from your file system and empty your trash

Retention guidance

  • Do not store assessment files on a shared drive unless access is restricted to authorised personnel.
  • Clear drafts from the browser after completing each engagement.
  • Retain exported files only as long as required by your contractual obligations or applicable rules.

GDPR note

If your assessment includes personal data (names, job titles, contact details of your client's staff), you — the consultant — are the data controller for that personal data. You must:

  • Ensure a lawful basis for the processing (typically contractual or legitimate interest tied to the engagement).
  • Inform the individuals whose data you process, per GDPR Articles 13/14.
  • Not retain personal data beyond its purpose.
  • Apply appropriate technical and organisational measures (see the risk notes above).

The tool is a data-processing aid; Obsidian Corps does not process any data you enter into it.
